# Product Strategy

## Strategic focus

Karri will begin with one repeated job: help a diaspora sender find a suitable traveler on the same cross-border corridor and coordinate the package responsibly. The early product should make this job easier and more legible than informal group chats without pretending to solve every logistics problem.

## Target segments

1. **Diaspora senders** moving personal, non-prohibited packages to family or community contacts.
2. **Diaspora travelers** with spare luggage capacity who are willing to carry a clearly described package for a stated reward.
3. **Recipients** who need predictable handoff and delivery confirmation; a dedicated recipient experience comes later.

## Product wedge

The first wedge is exact corridor liquidity. A shipment from one city/country pair to another is useful only when a compatible trip exists. The MVP therefore makes listing creation and corridor visibility the core loop:

1. Publish a clear shipment or trip.
2. Return to see owned listings.
3. Discover exact route matches.
4. Learn where the flow is unclear or demand is thin.

## Phased differentiation

- **Foundation:** authentication, profiles, listings, exact matching, safe data rules.
- **Coordination:** booking requests, acceptance, notifications, and participant visibility.
- **Accountability:** custody events, delivery evidence, reviews, and explainable trust signals.
- **Scale:** configurable corridor policies, operational tooling, localization, and measured geographic expansion.

## Measures

Early metrics should answer product questions, not reward vanity:

- Listing completion rate by role.
- Share of active listings with at least one exact match.
- Time from listing creation to first viable match.
- Match-to-booking-request conversion after booking exists.
- Booking completion and custody-event completeness after tracking exists.
- Repeat listing rate and reported safety incidents.

## Explicit non-goals for the current slice

Payments, disputes, chat, SMS, admin tooling, AI ranking, and complex trust algorithms are outside the current implementation. Their absence keeps the first learning loop small and auditable.
