# Business Model

## Value exchange

Senders gain access to travel capacity on routes that may be expensive or inconvenient through traditional carriers. Travelers can receive a clearly stated reward for using available luggage capacity. Karri creates value by making discovery, agreement, custody, and reputation more trustworthy.

## Revenue hypothesis

The leading long-term hypothesis is a transparent platform service fee charged only when a booking is accepted or completed. The fee model is not implemented in the MVP and should not be committed until corridor research answers:

- Whether senders or travelers are more willing to pay.
- Whether a fixed, percentage, or capped fee is easiest to understand.
- What support, insurance, payment, tax, and regulatory costs accompany a transaction.
- Whether rewards and fees need corridor-specific bounds.

The listing and exact-matching foundation does not collect money.

## Marketplace health

Karri should manage for completed, trustworthy journeys rather than raw listings. A healthy corridor has enough current shipments and trips, clear response expectations, low cancellation, complete custody evidence, and repeat participation on both sides.

## Cost structure

Early costs include Firebase usage, app distribution, identity and safety vendors when introduced, customer support, content/policy localization, and legal or compliance review. Managed services reduce infrastructure staffing but do not remove the need for operational controls.

## Expansion economics

New corridors should open only after validating demand and readiness. Feature flags and remote configuration support gradual rollout; they do not justify activating a corridor without policy, support, and prohibited-item guidance.

## Principles

- Fees must be visible before commitment.
- Trust and safety controls are not premium add-ons.
- Karri does not sell private custody or identity data.
- Incentives must not encourage undisclosed or prohibited items.
- Revenue decisions must not obscure who is responsible at each stage.
