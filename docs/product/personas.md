# Personas

Personas are decision tools, not demographic stereotypes. Research should refine their needs and language within each launch corridor.

## Amina — diaspora sender

Amina lives in the Washington, DC area and regularly helps family in Nairobi. She occasionally needs to send clothing, documents, or small personal items. She has used friends-of-friends but worries about unclear timing and repeated follow-up.

**Goals**

- Know whether anyone is traveling along the right route.
- Describe the package and reward once, clearly.
- Understand who has the package and when delivery is expected.

**Concerns**

- Whether the traveler is genuine and dependable.
- Whether the item is acceptable to carry.
- Whether private contact or package information is exposed too early.

## Daniel — diaspora traveler

Daniel travels between Toronto and Addis Ababa several times a year and sometimes has unused luggage capacity. He is willing to help if expectations, package contents, handoff timing, and compensation are clear.

**Goals**

- Find compatible requests without sorting through unrelated messages.
- Control how much weight he offers and which package he accepts.
- Avoid surprises at pickup, customs, or delivery.

**Concerns**

- Undisclosed or prohibited contents.
- Last-minute changes and unreliable handoffs.
- Being held responsible for claims he never accepted.

## Hana — recipient

Hana receives a package at the destination. She may not have created the shipment or have a Karri account in the first release, but her availability determines whether the final handoff succeeds.

**Goals**

- Know the expected handoff window.
- Verify the correct package and acknowledge receipt.
- Contact the right participant when plans change.

## Product implications

- One account can act as sender, traveler, or both.
- Route and schedule clarity come before social features.
- Package descriptions and capacity must be explicit but privacy-minimized.
- Matching is an invitation to evaluate fit, not an assurance of safety.
- Recipient participation needs a later dedicated requirements pass.
